Pointless Puzzle

I decided to have a bit of fun and set up a gauntlet of puzzles for anyone to enjoy.

The format is simple, each page will contain a puzzle that will lead to a word or code. Add this word to the end of the URL:

“jackhaydock.co.uk/puzzle/”

For example, if I gave you the riddle;

What walks on four legs in the morning,
two in the afternoon,
three in the evening
and none at night?

You would get “jackhaydock.co.uk/puzzle/man/”

Then type that into the address bar in your browser and that will take you to another page where you will see a letter or number and another puzzle. Note the letter/number down and try the next puzzle. Once you get all the characters, you’ll be able to spell something, e-mail me (jwhaydock@jackhaydock.co.uk) your answer and if you’re right, I’ll let you know.

Notes:

  • All URL additions will be all lower-case with no spaces, hyphens, underscores or other non-alphanumeric characters.
  • You will not be reshown the answer characters or given links back to them so make sure to keep note of them.
  • Please don’t reveal any answers to other visitors, let them play for themselves.
  • The final answer will be comprehendable, not just a random series of letters and numbers.
  • The format of the puzzles will be greatly varied but the same style (e.g. riddles) may appear multiple times.
  • Some puzzles will have multiple stages.
  • If you believe you have the right answer but can’t get the correct URL, contact me with your answer, if you are close enough to the correct answer I’ll give you the exact addition.
